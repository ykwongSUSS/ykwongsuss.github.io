df-regex

  • Regex works for strings, and is supported by stringr (part of TidyVerse)

  • Stringr uses its own match syntax (str_extract) , and it can be re-written equivalently using regex syntax

2. Replace

s1 <- c("The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog")
str_replace(s1, "lazy", "dozy")
[1] "The quick brown fox jumps over the dozy dog"

2a. str_subset to retrieve the matching rows

Replacing an “errant” symbol (%) in text

# find the symbol
str_subset(df$url,"\\%")
